Wireframes are lightweight, rapidly developed design specs that outline the structure and layout of a web page. In this reading, you will learn how to identify HTML elements from a wireframe spec and use it to generate semantic HTML code.

HTML, CSS, JS

Webpages are comprised of three major components:

  • HTML: Page structure, layout
  • CSS: Look and feel, styling and positioning
  • JavaScript: Interactivity, utility

Reading wireframes

Take a look at this floorplan for a house. The house's structure and layout are clearly measured while details like sinks, countertops and windows are represented with generic icons. The look and feel of those details depend on the interior designer but the architect is only concerned with their location.
